Animal Crossing is a game series that's been around since 2001. The latest incarnation, Animal Crossing: New Horizons, was released on March 20th, 2020. I started this game series with Animal Crossing: New Leaf (2005), and I've been hooked since.

When I first played Animal Crossing: New Leaf, I immediately became enthralled with all of the cute villagers and ability to manipulate the town into any image I saw fit. Animal Crossing: New Horizons is all that and much more. While multiplayer has always been available in this game series, New Horizons makes it even easier to connect with friends via online play.

Since CoVid-19 has made going outside and hanging out with friends pretty difficult, I have been hosting "parties" in my town on Animal Crossing: New Horizons. I invite a few of my closest friends to a zoom call and we all dress in the weeks theme and sip some adult beverages.

While I usually just involve myself with a theme for people to dress their characters in, I had an idea one night to do a scavenger hunt. I thought of countless ways to create such a minigame on my island but I realized it would be difficult to hide single items for a group of people and still keep track of progress per person. This is when I had the idea to create a web app that everyone could launch and play along.

The Game works like this;

  • The player visits my town through the online service provided in-game.
  • The player then opens the web-app
  • in the app the player recieves hints as to where they should be looking for the 3 digit code associated with the hint
  • in the town there are written numbers hidden associated with their hint
  • the player finds the number and inputs it into the app
  • once the player has checked all of the hints, the app will tell them where to go for their prize
